# 🚀 Tech Trends Shaping 2024: Teams, Power, Automation & Remote Work  

Hargreaves Lansdown’s **investment in senior tech talent** shows that even traditional finance firms recognise the need for *strategic leadership* to drive digital transformation (1). At the same time, the Bangkok Post reminds us that **data‑centre growth is only as strong as its power infrastructure** – a reminder that scaling cloud services demands robust, sustainable electricity (2).  

The two Activist Post pieces raise a **cultural counter‑point**: while we celebrate smarter cars and automation, we must ask whether we’re automating *meaningless* tasks (4) and whether the narrative that “your car was never the target” distracts from broader systemic issues (3).  

Finally, Sam Altman’s warning that the industry’s “full‑remote forever” experiment may have been a **mistake** forces leaders to rethink hybrid models that preserve flexibility without eroding collaboration (5).  

**Bottom line:** 2024 will be defined by balancing *talent, infrastructure, purposeful automation, and a nuanced return‑to‑office strategy*.
